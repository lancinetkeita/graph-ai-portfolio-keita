Introduction

About the Demographic and Health Survey (DHS):

The Demographic and Health Surveys (DHS) are a series of standardized surveys conducted periodically in developing countries that collect data on various aspects of population, health, and nutrition.

About DHS data used for the report:

This final data analysis project will be conducted on the individual recode data set for Guinea. The dataset contains individual-level information on men and women aged 15 to 49 in the surveyed households. This analysis focuses on the women data.

Year of fieldwork: 2018

Full data set for Guinea: 10,874 women

About the analyses

This project will investigate factors that may influence or affect a womans likelihood to use modern contraception in Guinea.

Contraception Trends

According to the WHO, modern contraceptive methods include hormonal methods (pills, injectables, implants), intrauterine devices (IUDs), condoms, and sterilization. These methods are highly effective in preventing unintended pregnancies.

By this definition, the modern contraceptive prevalence rate (mCPR) among women in union aged 15-49 in Guinea is relatively low.

Place of residence and education

When compared to women in rural areas, women in urban areas are more likely to achieve higher levels of education and use modern contraception.

Age and contraception

Older women who have more children are more likely to use modern contraception, likely due to completed family size.

Wealth and contraception

Women from wealthier households are more likely to use modern contraception, reflecting better access to health services.

Conclusion

The analysis explored factors influencing womens use of modern contraception in Guinea. Key patterns emerged:

Higher likelihood of use among women in urban areas, with higher education, greater household wealth, and better access to health services.

Lower likelihood of use among women in rural areas, with little or no education, lower household wealth, and limited health service access.

Regional disparities were evident, underscoring the need for targeted interventions in areas with low contraceptive prevalence.

About Guinea

The Republic of Guinea is located in West Africa. It shares borders with Guinea-Bissau, Senegal, Mali, Cte dIvoire, Liberia, and Sierra Leone. Guinea has a population of approximately 13 million people and is rich in natural resources, particularly bauxite, iron ore, and gold.

The country faces significant health challenges, including low contraceptive prevalence rates and high maternal and child mortality. Improving access to family planning services remains a priority for the countrys health system.
